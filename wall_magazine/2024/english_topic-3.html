Floral Demise

~ Cadet Roman, 2464



1.
Pin drop silence in the huge empty hall room – except for the screeching sound chalk swiftly being scribbled with. The madam of “Axiom Astrophysics Laboratory” with the “Einstein like” hair weaving the white apron in filling the gigantic background with tons of gibberish equations, symbols and variables, Lily smiles and enters the room with soft treads. She says, “Dad, come on. You didn’t sleep ever last night. You should get some rest.” Professor Neil Franzberg stops scribbling and faces towards her with a wise smirk, ”My dear daughter , you see this variables in the equation? They are like socred flowers stuck inside rubbles . I must save them at all costs.” Meanwhile he pulls out a Lily rose from inside of his apron and says, ”Beautiful, isn’t it?” Lily smirks and replies, “Would be an understatement.” Neil is working on the theory of multiverse and the entanglement among them. Everyone acquinted with him and said he’d been trying to materialize fairy tales so stubbornly. But he always had passion for physics. He even cracked a joke on it, ”Physics ought to be my second wife!” For the love for his intangible “Second wife”, he has spent most of his life scribbling on the blackboard. Like father like son, Lily just couldn’t but fall in love with his father’s works. So she has become a physicist as well.

Few months letter, Lily went to his father’s hallroom in the morning with a cup of tea as always. But to her utter surprise when she entered the room, Neil wasn’t scribbling on the board. He was lying down with eyes open. She promptly rushed to him and checked his pulse. He is alive but – vaguely alive. He is taken to hospital and it was found out that he’s having internal bleeding inside his heart. But it’s too late. Beside his deathbed, Lily was there, holding his hand tightly. Neil’s lost words there. “Please…don’t….kill……them”. Life couldn’t fohtom who were “them”, but she promised to herself that she would prove her father’s theory.


2.
In the third planet of 5 dimensional solar system named earth, a huge tree can see pandemonium all around. His flowers are falling apart one by one. He cries, “Please don’t kell them.” But chainsaw is starting at him in utter thirst. It listen to his gibberish plea. With its sharp grooves, it pulls the souls out of the trees. The cropses of the innocent victims lie on the ground.

Suddenly Lily wakes up-thinking it was a nightmare. Yes, it was. But more importantly, it was a crossroad in her journey.

After tiredless efforts of 9 years, Professor Lily Lowndes has solved her father’s equation. She found out- In the vast spectrum of billions of ralities in quintillions of multiverse, we decide where we end up to. We are the choosers of our own multiverse. Neil had no connection with that tree in another universe. But the fact is , multiverses are very delicately and linearly entangled where a minescule of difference of choice can lead someone into a whole different universe.

Either we choose a multiverse where see rubbles all around and can not find any flower underneath them – as we’ve ruined the ABODE of FLOWERS. Or, we choose a multiverse where children of our future generation talk in silence flowers for hours. They don’t need to search for them inside rubbles. That is the multiverse we want to step into.
