I'll Wait

~ Cadet Azean, 2263


How long it’s been? It’s hard to keep track of time down here. Well, it doesn’t matter actually. I have a strong feeling that you would come to meet me today. You will look as beautiful as the first time we met. Some people never miss their charm. You’re one of them. Do you remember how we met for the first time? I know you remember it all, but I will tell you again, as many times as possible. Our HSC exam was over, I along with my friends had lots of free time and very less work to do. It was 7th of June. That was the day I saw you for the first time- walking by the road in a blue saree, the wind blew your hairs away and you were trying to straighten them with your hand. I stood awestruck, motionless. Your picture was engraved in my heart at that moment. You are someone whom I had never known, but someone who was dearer to me than anyone else. Days started passing quickly. I joined Bangladesh Military Academy as an officer cadet. You went to Chattogram University. We were in the same city, breathing the same air, but aeons of distance prevailed between us. I always wondered, “Does she miss me as much as I do?” till now I don’t have any answer to that question, but it’s okay. Some questions are best left unanswered. Every time I came to vacation, my first task was to meet you. Every time, you got a bit angry and asked me, “How many times have I told you to bring a flower while meeting me? If you can’t get a flower, don’t come to meet me.” Every time I apologized and promised to bring one next time. But I never brought any flower. Why should I miss my chance to see your pretty face turn reddish in anger and to hear your sweet voice tremble with pretty anger? I don’t have any flower with me today as well. Will you get angry today? Days passed quickly, I passed out from BMA. I got my first posting as a Lieutenant in Rangpur Cantonment. We spent so many times chatting over the phone. After I was promoted to Captain, I was transferred to Ramu Cantonment of Cox’s Bazar. The rest of the story is not mine or yours, it’s ours. With the consent of both the families, we married. Our story wasn’t a tragic one, rather one with the most beautiful ending that I could never imagine.

Even one month didn’t pass after our marriage, I was asked to join the UN Peace Keeping Mission mission in Chad. You know call of duty can’t be ignored. Your ears burnt my heart, but I couldn’t back off at that time. I was bound by an oath and I couldn’t but fulfill it. On the day of my departure, you came to the airport with me to bid me farewell in the blue saree that you wore on the day we first met. Maybe tears are contagious; I felt two drops of warm water rolling down my eyes after you broke into tears. Before boarding on the plane, I asked you one question, “Will you patiently wait for me?” you somehow managed a smile and nodded, without any verbal answer. I know you still wait for me to return. Even after knowing that it’s impossible for me to return, you wait patiently. That’s why every once in a while, you come and visit my grave, wearing the same blue saree that you wore on the day we met. I never brought flowers for you, but whatever you come to meet me, you bring flowers for me.

I wish I could live with you for eternity, but fate didn’t want that maybe. No, it wasn’t luck, it was totally my fault. When the rebels threw the grenade at the peacekeepers, I could have stayed put, and run away to safety. But it was my choice to lie on the grenade and cover for them. It was my choice to die. Sorry my beloved angel, I left you alone. I couldn’t just leave my fellow comrade to die in front of me. My death was not my sacrifice, it was yours and mine- it was ours. Our sacrifice stopped many more sacrifices from being made.

I can see you in front of my grave, wearing blue saree with a blue flower in your hands. See, my gut feelings turn out to be quite accurate. I want to tell so many things with you. But I know you can hear nothing, it’s okay. Your sole presence makes me happy. No, no …… don’t cry. Just put the flowers on my grave, like you always do. Someday you cross the wall that separates me from you. Till then, I’ll wait for you, as you are waiting for me for so many days.
