Beyond the Screen

~ Cadet Abdullah, 2223



What’s happening is like a nightmare. Sameer is standing in front of his television and the breaking news goes thus “Sudden internet blackout all over the globe due to E-COVID2023”.
“This doesn’t make any sense!” he mutters. He searches on Google about this “E-COVID” thing, but the screen keeps showing ‘Error 404’.
He desperately changes the channels on the TV and stops at one-
“E-COVID2023 is a recently invented malware which has infected every single website and app online resulting in a complete internet breakdown overnight. It’s like a digital pandemic leaving the whole world at a deadlock. But nobody knows where it came from all of a sudden.”
He sighs and sets out for his university. He studies at MIT. He’s shocked to see most of the departments, including the one he’s in, being closed due to the digital pandemic. Such a mess it is!
After coming back home, he is supposed to make a video call to his parents back in Bangladesh as per his daily routine. But he just can’t. It really feels like a lockdown in COVID-19; or something even worse than that.
He can’t scroll on Facebook, or watch YouTube, or text his friends and the list goes on. He lays on his bed and stares at the ceiling with utter somberness. He wanders around in his mind, “Perhaps Zuckerberg or Jeff Bezos is lying on their bed just like me!”
In the afternoon he goes shopping but returns empty handed. Because the credit cards or any sort of online transaction system are now useless. He turns the TV on. Hundreds of millions of people became jobless, the world economy is facing the biggest depression of history and almost the entire education system has collapsed. Everyone from the bottom to top the tier is facing a more or less similar crisis.
Amidst all these massacres, he goes out for a walk. The butterflies and bees are buzzing over the bloomed roses, dancing in the breeze. The silent serenades’ echoing all over the garden.
He’s been in this apartment for about a year, yet he never noticed that the beautiful garden just beside his lawn. Obviously these did not just appear from thin air. He goes to the lake nearby and witness a glimpse of twilight. The sun was just over the horizon. All along the evening, he stays lying on the grass looking up in the sky counting stars. For the first time ever, he can see the constellations in front of his eyes which he saw on his textbook or perhaps on his mobile screen. The Pulsar, Orion and Cassipia- those were never this much vivid to him. In fact, those did exist but he was blind to them for so long.
Coming back home, while he is having his dinner, notifications bombarded his phone and he realized that the internet has been restored. However, instead of scrolling his news feed like he used to do he puts his phone on aero plane mode and focuses on having his dinner.
The E-COVID lasted for about 12 hours but taught him a lifelong lesson. A 6 inch window can never let you view the bigger picture of life.
