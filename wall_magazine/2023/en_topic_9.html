The Scream in Turkey

~ Cadet Samin, 2284


Nature is the spectacle of continuous metamorphosis- sometimes bewitching as the butterflies getting out of the cocoons, but ghastly when it flies into rage with its raw holocaust, exploding the allure, turning into a lump of trauma. She is nature, endure of her own. A grievous rage of the nature breezed in and turned into a catastrophe on the most recent days- the earthquake in Turkey.

The city of Gaziantep, Kahramanmaras is a province of Turkey. The most powerful Muslim nation faced a merciless hecatomb of nature on 6th February. 2023. A sudden earthquake of 7.8 degrees in the Richter scale, shook the whole city. Gaziantep being the epicenter, spread the collateral damage to the southern and central Turkey. Syria, the neighbor of Turkey also became part of the disaster. People lost their lives, assets and reasons to survive in such a traumatic condition.

It was hardly nine hours after the first blow. People were still lost in the trauma, it was far from being recovered. Another quake of 7.7 degrees shook an area 95km north from the previous epicenter. Both of the quakes raptured Turkey as well as Syria. According to BBC, this was the deadliest earthquake in the history of the earth since 2010 Haiti earthquake. A catastrophe engraved its name in the history of the world.

About 41.100 people lost their lives as per the statistics presented up to 14th February. Among them, 35,400 were from Turkey and 5700 were from Syria. 13.5 million people were affected. 105,500 people were injured. 4 million buildings were damaged. These numbers cross the limits of trauma for the whole world.

The disaster brought with itself drastic changes in the geographical state of Turkey. The stimulations in the earth crust amassed large snow to fall from the hillsides. People are at a great risk of hypothermia. The epicenter is situated at the East Anatolian Fault-Line which is 700 km long. It puts a vast effect on the earth surface.

Despite the nature’s cruelty, consciousness could always help minimizing the effects. That is all we can do. The souls should gather, working hand in hand for the retrieval. It’s a disaster for the humanity. It’s the duty of us all to face it.
