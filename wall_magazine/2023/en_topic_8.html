The Man Who Touched the Sky

~ Cadet Ahmed, 2199


Name: Fazlur Rahman Khan.
Nationality: Bangladeshi.
Ancestral Home: Vandarikandi, Madaripur.
Birth: 3 April, 1929.
Death: 27th March, 1982.

F.R. Khan was a Bangladeshi-American structural engineer and a renowned architect. He was a pioneer of Computer-aided Design (CAD) and is famously known as the father of tubular designs. From a small colony of the British Empire, the emergence of such an architectural genius is undoubtedly an astonishing story.


F.R. Khan was born in Dhaka, His father, Khan Bahadur Abdur Rahman Khan was a high school math teacher and a textbook author. He was appointed as the Director of Public Instruction in Bengal and later became the first principal of Jagannath College. His mother, Khadiza Khatun was the daughter of the Zaminder of Dulai region of Pabna.


F.R. Khan completed his secondary education in Arranitda Government High School. He studied in the subject of Civil Engineering from Bengal Engineering & Science University., Shibpur, Kolkata. He achieved Bachelor degree of Civil Engineering.


The architectural maestro, left foot marks by the remarkable career. In 1955 Dr. F.R. Khan joined a consulting farm Skidmone, Owings and Merrle. He left USA in 1957. But he returned USA in 1960 again and became a significant partner of SOM in 1966, earning marvelous reputations for his excellence.


This engineering pioneer, had his name quitted to glory by his works. He improved the application of frame shear wall interaction system. His innovative idea about tube structural system created an evolutionary change in world of construction. He was the one to pioneer the lightweight concrete in sky sorapers. For his innovative use of structural system that are fundamental to modern skyscraper and designed constructions, he was given the greatest structural engineer award of 20th century.


This architectural magician designed the sears tower, which is called Willis Tower now. It was the tallest building from 1973 to 1998 in the whole world. He was the architect of the 100 story John Hancock Center.


The first building to employ tube structure, Chestnut De-Witt apartment building was built under his planning. He had his personal papers held by Ryerson and Burnham Libraries at Art Institution of Chicago. He had a complete different conception in the world of engineering lighting up the pathway for the juveniles. He was the one to show the world of Engineering from a broad perspective of life. He said, “The technological man must not be lost in his own technology. He must be able to appreciate life and life art, drama, music and most importantly people.”

His way of thinking and excellence earned him the title “fellow architect” by the greatest Bruce Graham.


F.R. Khan, known as the Einstein of structural engineering passed his personal life with harmony. He and his wife liselotte Khan tied with the responsibilities of a daughter named Yasmin Sabina Khan in 1960. He attained his US citizenship in 1967.


The artist of structural engineering was a fellow patriot supporting the country collection aids and opinions during liberation war. His exceptional way of thinking, knowledge and ability accompanied by the wonderful communication with people. Such a humble personality was never indignant of this achievements.


A piece of excellence conquering the world from such an abandoned land back then. No one could ever think of a mastermind from such a dark corner in the colony of Europe. He is the pride of our country, inspiration of million juveniles. A name to be always remembered.
