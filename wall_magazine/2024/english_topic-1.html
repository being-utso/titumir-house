Plumeria

~ Cadet Jihad, 2328



In the vast enchantment of floral tapestry, the poet admires his roses and their crimson petals; their magical aura and the honeybees circling around them. Their aroma mesmerizes even the most secluded corner of the garden. Their petals are drenched in the ocean of loves of poets just like him. Then thorns are the vanguards; protecting the Gilgamesh of love stories written on their petals for Millenials that witnessed from Caeser- Cleopetra to Napoleon – Josephine; and Romeo – Juliet, transcending reality. With all its grandeur, the roses chorus the sweet serenade of love. Butterflies and drones join them in this beautiful orchestra and the daisies, buttercups and tulips unleash their own charms and spectra. Amidst myriad colours and surrealism, the poet looks for the most seemingly somber yet sacred part of the garden. Plumeria sits in silence, in utter delicacy. It does not chorus in the serenade. It does not have vibrant petals – except for the smooth golden yellow gradient in the middle of its off-white petals. It doesn’t even have an intoxicating fragrance, neither honeybees circle around them nor butterflies befriend with them. It has been sitting in that very secluded corner of the garden for years; in such simplicity, in such minimalism. In fact, it had nothing the roses had, but it was everything that roses could not be. The poet comes closer to it, and a gentle breezes washes away its loneliness. “Their” loneliness. He plucks one of the Plumerias from millions of the blossom. A shaft of sunrays beams down on its small petals; on his little infinity of love. He reads and writes for thousand years in the white pages of Plumeria embalmed in his tears.

Plumeria was always simple to him until one day – he found it to be the loveliest thing he even had, and will ever have. Faded in oblivions, his memories embedded themselves in the favourite flower of ‘his’ flower. For, the poet cries in ache, “Dear you were the Plumeria in the garden of my heart….”
