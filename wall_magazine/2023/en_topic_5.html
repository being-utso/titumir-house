They Whispered Me to Death

~ Cadet Tripto, 2283



They twinkled at the north,
And treacherously became a blitz of Erebus.
They dreamt me the Nile,
Leaving in the realm of cactus.
They brailed hatred in this heart of Eros,
And read it with their thirsty swords.
My half still holds the accolade of love
The half that forgot uttering misery in words
They abseiled climbing me to the peak
Edicted for salvation,
With the coldest stroke.
Like my death was just a filament
It was my heart,
Before my arms broke.
They built me in years,
Demolished in seconds.
I was indignant of my laws,
Abridged them, who amends.
I accorded them with pen,
Was answered with a knife.
I stood up on my knees,
To stumble my whole life
O Caravan, O Caravan!
Flow my with the Nile,
Where the crystals of love collides
With the dark deep ocean of faith
O Caravan, O Caravan!
They whispered me to death.
