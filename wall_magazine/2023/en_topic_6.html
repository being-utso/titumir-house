The Metamorphosis

~ Cadet Maruf, 2254



Name of the Book: The Metamorphosis
Author: Franz Kafka
Publication Year: 1915

Metamorphosis is a highly autobiographical novella written by the Prague writer, Franz Kafka. Kafka’s own life and own family was the model for the life of the protagonist OF THE NOVELLA, Gregor Samsa and his family.
The word, metamorphosis defined in terms of change- physical, emotional or spiritual. In the novella, “The Metamorphosis”, the same change has been displayed.
“When Gregor Samsa woke up one morning from unsettling dreams, he found himself changed in his bed into a monstrous vermin.”
Kafka opens the simple yet vastly meaningful novella with the above sentence, a simple statement of fact. The protagonist, Gregor Samsa, is a travelling salesman employed by an unnamed manager. As the only earning member of his family, Gregor has to carry on his job despite disliking it. As the opening sentence states, one day, the protagonist finds himself transformed into a ‘monstrous vermin’. What is referred to by the word vermin is unclear, but many critics suggest its meaning as a millipede or centipede like insect. This metamorphosis renders him useless for his job. Thus, the attitude of his family gradually changes towards him. The person who bore his family for so long suddenly became a burden for his entire family. His sister, Grete took care of him for sometimes. But due to various incidents, Samsa in the end becomes hated and despised by her too. This feeling of aloneness drove him to commit suicide and his family exhaled a sigh of relief.
Gregor Samsa has always wanted to escape from his job but couldn’t managed to do so due to his social responsibility. However, his metamorphosis enabled him to do so, giving him his desired freedom. But this freedom cost his family members’ affection and his acceptance into society. This is the existential crisis that Gregor, as well as most of the human beings of this modern era have to face. This crisis is the conflict between the desire of freedom of a man on one hand and the other responsibilities imposed by the society and family on the other.
‘The Metamorphosis’ is extremely influenced by existential philosophy. This philosophy maintains that without freedom of choice, responsibility or moral individualism, life has no significance. Many people have to continue to live on with the pains and sufferings of this world being bound by the responsibilities imposed by the society against their own desire for freedom. But Gregor Samsa, realizing that freedom to make choices about his own life is trust form of existence of a human being, the one decision that freed him from all the hatred and despise and solved his existential crisis.
Another theme found in this novella is the conflict between bourgeoisie and the proletariat. While the unnamed manager of Gregor Samsa’s shop represents the bourgeoisie class, to whom the members of working class has no value except for their works, Gregor himself is a representation of the working class. Gregor had value only when he could do work. The moment he turned into a vermin, he lost every bit of value he held in this capitalist society.
